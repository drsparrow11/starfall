# Prelude Before the Sky

Before the sky had a name
before the stars knew where to fall
there was a song in the room
too quiet to call

There was breath in the brass
there was dust on the keys
there were hands learning time
there were pages learning me

I did not start with lightning
I did not start with flame
I started with a little sound
that would not say my name

There was music before the sky
there was a signal under everything
I was listening before I knew why
I was carrying what I could not sing

Before the star
before the wire
before the answer came alive

There was music before the sky

There were folders full of almost
there were melodies in sleep
there were old unfinished doorways
I was too afraid to keep

There were rooms where drums were thunder
there were reeds that learned to cry
there were lines across the paper
trying to teach my hands to fly

I did not need a miracle
to prove the ache was real
I only needed somewhere
for the hidden thing to heal

There was music before the sky
there was a signal under everything
I was listening before I knew why
I was carrying what I could not sing

Before the star
before the wire
before the answer came alive

There was music before the sky

Amber in the margins
Jade beneath the line
two little lights
I drew before their time

Every note a window
every rest a room
every almost waiting
for a later moon

there was a signal under everything
I was listening before I knew why
I was carrying what I could not sing

Before the star

before the answer came alive

There was music before the sky

before the sky
